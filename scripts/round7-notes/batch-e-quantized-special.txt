Batch was 10 binaries: 1 audio binary (whisper-microphone) and 9 quantized-* GGUF binaries. After surveying fuel-core/src/lazy_*.rs, only TWO quantized lazy modules ship: lazy_quantized_smollm3 (SmolLM3-only, has full GGUF loader) and lazy_quantized_whisper (Whisper-only, from_gguf stubbed with explicit 'not yet wired' error). None of the 10 binaries in this batch overlap with those — quantized maps to LLaMA-family (incl. SmolLM2 not SmolLM3), quantized-gemma to Gemma3, quantized-glm4 to GLM-4, quantized-lfm2 to LFM2 (no F32 port either), quantized-phi to Phi-2/Phi-3, quantized-qwen2-instruct to Qwen2, quantized-qwen3 to Qwen3, quantized-qwen3-moe to Qwen3-MoE, quantized-t5 to T5. Each would need a per-architecture GGUF→WeightStorage::Q4_0 loader that doesn't exist. whisper-microphone separately needs sampling + KV cache + no-speech detection + language detection scaffolding the lazy_whisper module doesn't ship. Conclusion: this entire batch is bottlenecked on lazy-side infrastructure (GGUF loaders + sampler/KV plumbing), not on main.rs rewrites. Cleanest outcome is per-binary skip reasons documenting the specific lazy gap; success=true per task spec since all gaps are honest infrastructure absences rather than 'too complex to think about'. Side note: while smoke-testing baseline compilation, I discovered fuel-transformers/src/models/llm/deepseek2.rs has a missing HostBuffer::I8 match arm that pre-dates this batch and breaks all eager examples on this branch; I patched it locally to verify the eager baselines compiled, then reverted (out of scope for this batch and crosses into sibling-project territory per cross_project_changes guidance). The deepseek2.rs nonzero op needs an I8 arm in the upstream fix.
